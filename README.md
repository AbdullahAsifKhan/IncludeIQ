# IncludeIQ: AI Chief Accessibility Officer

## Tagline

An AI powered workplace accessibility intelligence agent that identifies inclusion risks, explains their impact, and generates evidence backed action plans before employees are affected.

---

## The Problem

More than 1.3 billion people worldwide live with a disability. Yet most organizations report only a small percentage of employees as having disabilities, despite research showing that the true number is much higher.

The issue is not that organizations intentionally exclude people. The problem is that accessibility barriers are often hidden inside everyday workplace processes.

* A job description may unintentionally discourage qualified candidates from applying.
* A meeting process may exclude employees who rely on captions or asynchronous communication.
* An onboarding document may not work properly with screen readers.
* An accommodation policy may be outdated or difficult to navigate.

These issues are rarely discovered until an employee experiences them directly.

Large organizations generate thousands of documents, policies, job postings, and meeting records every month. HR teams and managers cannot realistically review everything manually. As a result, accessibility gaps remain invisible until they become employee complaints, retention problems, compliance issues, or legal risks.

Organizations need a way to continuously identify and address accessibility barriers before they cause harm.

---

## Our Solution

IncludeIQ is an AI Chief Accessibility Officer for modern organizations.

It continuously analyzes workplace documents, communications, and policies to identify accessibility and disability inclusion risks across the organization.

Instead of acting as a simple compliance checker, IncludeIQ reasons across multiple sources of information to uncover patterns, prioritize risks, and recommend actions that improve workplace inclusion.

The goal is simple:

**Help organizations discover and fix accessibility barriers before employees are negatively impacted.**

---

## Microsoft IQ Architecture

IncludeIQ combines Work IQ, Foundry IQ, and Fabric IQ to create an end to end accessibility intelligence workflow.

### Work IQ: Workplace Understanding

Work IQ provides workplace context and understanding.

It analyzes:

* Job descriptions
* Meeting transcripts
* HR policies
* Onboarding documents
* Internal communications

Work IQ identifies language, workflows, and communication patterns that may create accessibility barriers for employees with disabilities.

### Foundry IQ: Grounded Accessibility Intelligence

For every potential issue identified, Foundry IQ retrieves trusted accessibility guidance and supporting evidence.

Examples include:

* ADA guidance
* WCAG accessibility standards
* EEOC recommendations
* Internal accessibility policies
* Disability inclusion frameworks

Every recommendation is grounded in citations and evidence, reducing hallucinations and increasing trust.

### Fabric IQ: Organizational Reasoning

Fabric IQ enables organization wide reasoning.

Instead of analyzing documents individually, it identifies recurring patterns across teams, departments, and workflows.

Examples include:

* Repeated meeting accessibility issues
* Consistent hiring barriers across departments
* Organization wide policy gaps
* Recurring onboarding accessibility challenges

This transforms accessibility from a document level problem into an organizational intelligence problem.

## Example

A company asks:

*"Where are we most likely creating barriers for employees with disabilities?"*

IncludeIQ responds:

**Accessibility Score:** 68/100

### Highest Risk Areas

**1. Meeting Accessibility**

* 74% of analyzed meetings lacked captions

**2. Hiring Process**

* 31 job descriptions missing accommodation statements

**3. Onboarding Experience**

* Multiple onboarding documents not compatible with screen readers

### Recommended Actions

1. Enable automatic captions for all meetings
2. Standardize accommodation language across hiring materials
3. Update onboarding templates for assistive technology compatibility

Each recommendation includes supporting evidence and citations.

Most accessibility tools focus on websites and applications.

**"Is this website accessible?"**

IncludeIQ answers a much larger question:

Rather than auditing a single webpage, IncludeIQ evaluates how accessibility is experienced across hiring, communication, onboarding, and workplace operations.
